# Overcoming Institutional Friction

The fastest way to kill a national coordination project is to demand that institutions centralize all of their raw data. Bureaucracies will defensively cite privacy laws, operational independence, and security risks. 

To maneuver past institutional resistance, the architecture must be scoped around a **Minimal Viable Coordination** (MVC) parameter.

## 1. Minimal Scope: The Five Pillar Baseline
Instead of trying to ingest "the whole state," we begin with exactly 5 nodes that naturally represent independent signal ecosystems. Convergence is structurally impossible without them.

1. **Treasury/Finance Analytics** (Structural Economic Stress)
2. **Ministry of Health** (Human Population Stress)
3. **Police Operations** (Operational Physical Incidents)
4. **Energy/Telecom** (Critical Systems Strain)
5. **Executive Secretariat** (The Convening Authority)

## 2. Minimal Computation: The 2-of-5 Trigger
The system does not need complex Machine Learning to prove immediate value. It utilizes a highly defensible, transparent rule matrix:

*Trigger a coordination review when:*
1. At least **2 out of 5** domains show an `anomaly_score ≥ τ` in the same geographic region.
2. This alignment lasts for **2 consecutive windows** (Persistence).
3. At least one domain is definitively non-social (to prevent online discourse from triggering physical operations).

## 3. Minimal Operating Routine
We design an operational routine that perfectly mimics ordinary government processes rather than sci-fi surveillance systems.

* **Monday-Tuesday:** Institutions submit their normalized Signal Contracts.
* **Wednesday:** The Convergence Layer automates the Risk Briefs and queues the alerts.
* **Thursday:** Interagency executive review meeting convenes (30–45 mins).
* **Friday:** Tactical mitigations and responses are delegated back to the local institutions.

By implementing this Minimal Architecture, we achieve an irrefutable win: we establish **earlier cross-domain agreement on what deserves attention**, completely bypassing the political minefield of data collection.
